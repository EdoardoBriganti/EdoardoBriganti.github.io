    About

    I will soon join the Canadian Economic Analysis Department of the Bank of Canada as a Senior Economist.

    I received my PhD in Economics from UC San Diego in June 2024.

    I grew up in Brianza, studied Economics in Milan (Bocconi) and Victoria B.C. (UVic) and worked as an Investment Consultant in Phnom Penh. I graduated from Bocconi as a first generation college student, writing a dissertation in Economic Theory under the supervision of Professor Simone Cerreia-Vioglio. The dissertation was awarded a Giorgio Mortara Scholarship from the Bank of Italy. Before moving to San Diego to pursue my doctoral studies, I worked in Bocconi as a Research Assistant for Professor Carlo Favero. At UC San Diego I studied under the supervision of Professor Valerie Ramey and enjoyed the guidance, support and help of the macro faculty, like Giacomo Rondina, and, in particular my committee members: Jim Hamilton, Nir Jaimovich, Johannes Wieland and Munseob Lee .

    Research Interests: Macroeconomics, Fiscal and Monetary Policy, Production Networks and Public Economics.
