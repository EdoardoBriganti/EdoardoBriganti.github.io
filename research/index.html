Working papers

  • The Network Effects of Fiscal Adjustment Plans (2018) with Carlo Favero and Madina Karamysheva.
    PDF |
    Summary: We use spatial econometric techniques to study the propagation of fiscal adjustment plans throughout the industrial network in the US economy.
  • Spatial Panel Econometrics meets Macro: an illustration with production networks and fiscal shocks. (2020).
    Summary: Explain the role of the main diagonal and row-normalization when adopting spatial autoregression whereby the underlying network is determined by macro data.
  • Heterogeneous Effects of PIT shocks: a regional approach (2020) with Carlos Goes and Victor Sellemi.
    Summary: . we construct state/commuting zone level income distribution in order to construct region specific personal income tax shocks, after constructing local income distribuitions. We study their effect at a regional level on different outcomes, such as unemployment. In particular, we pick few exogenous marginal tax changes and employ local projections to calculate the impulse responses of those fiscal events on the regions.
  • Production Network Transmission of Government Spending Shocks: Evidence from the US. (2020).
    Summary: I exploit exogenous fiscal events and input-output data to provide evidence, as well as to quantify, the propagation of government spending shocks through the production network, in the US economy.
