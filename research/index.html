Working papers

  • The Network Effects of Fiscal Adjustment Plans (2018) with Carlo Favero and Madina Karamysheva .
    [PDF]
    Summary: We use spatial econometric techniques to study the propagation of fiscal adjustment plans throughout the industrial network in the US economy. We find that the stronger downstream propagation of tax based fiscal consolidations explains more than half of the smaller total effect of expenditure based fiscal adjsutments on the economy.
  • Spatial Panel Econometrics meets Macro: an illustration with production networks and fiscal shocks. (2020).
    Summary: I explore the role of the main diagonal and row-normalization of a network when adopting spatial autoregression techniques, whereby the underlying network is determined by macroeconomic data.
  • Heterogeneous Effects of PIT shocks: a regional approach (2020) with Carlos Goes and Victor Sellemi.
    Summary: . We construct state level income distribution with "Generalized Pareto Interpolation" in order to construct region specific personal income tax shocks. We chose some exogenous marginal tax changes and employ local projections to calculate the impulse responses of those fiscal events on some region specific outcome variables.
  • Production Network Transmission of Government Spending Shocks: Evidence from the US. (2020).
    Summary: I exploit exogenous fiscal events and input-output data to provide evidence, and quantify, the propagation of government spending shocks through the production network, in the US economy.
