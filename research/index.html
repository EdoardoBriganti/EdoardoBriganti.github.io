Working papers

  • Why Does GDP Move Before Government Spending? It's all in the Meaurement (2022) with Victor Sellemi. Reject&Resubmit at American Economic Review.
    [PDF] [Online Appendix]
    Summary: We find that the early impact of defense news shocks on GDP is due to a rise in business inventories, as contractors ramp up production for new defense contracts. These contracts do not affect government spending (G) until payment-on-delivery, which occurs 2-3 quarters later. Novel data on defense procurement obligations reveals that contract awards Granger-cause shocks to G identified via Cholesky decomposition, but not defense news shocks. We show that Cholesky shocks to G miss early changes in inventories, and thus result in lower multiplier estimates relative to the narrative method.
